# Verbatim Messaging & Copy Guide

## Voice Principles

The verbatim voice is:
- **Direct:** Get to the point. No fluff.
- **Plain:** Use everyday language. Avoid jargon.
- **Grounded:** Show, don't tell. Use concrete examples.
- **Confident but humble:** We know what we do. We don't oversell.
- **Human-first:** Always remember real people are involved.

## Writing Checklist

Before publishing any copy, ask:

‚ñ° Did I use any buzzwords? (Remove them)
‚ñ° Could this be shorter? (Make it shorter)
‚ñ° Would my audience say this? (Match their language)
‚ñ° Am I showing or telling? (Show with examples)
‚ñ° Did I explain what actually happens? (Be specific)
‚ñ° Would I find this helpful if I were the reader? (Be honest)

## Words We Use

**Prefer:**
- Talk / conversation / interview
- Find / discover / spot / see
- Themes / patterns
- People / respondents / participants
- Real / actual / genuine
- Understanding / insight / knowledge

**Avoid:**
- Leverage / utilize / unlock / transform
- Revolutionary / game-changing / paradigm shift
- Next-generation / cutting-edge
- Empower / enable (unless truly specific)
- Solutions / ecosystem / platform (when unnecessary)
- Synergy / holistic / strategic alignment

---

## Words We Don't Avoid (But Use Carefully)

**AI / Artificial Intelligence:**
- Use when relevant, but don't overuse
- Explain what the AI actually does
- Don't hide behind it or make it sound magical

**Scale / At scale:**
- Fine to use, but be specific about numbers when possible
- "Talk to hundreds" is better than "scale your research"

**Insights:**
- Use sparingly
- Often "findings" or "themes" is more specific

## The Verbatim "No-No" List

Never say:
- "Revolutionary" or "game-changing"
- "Unlock insights"
- "Leverage the power of"
- "Transform your organization"
- "Seamless integration"
- "Unparalleled" or "best-in-class"
- "Solutions" (unless you need to)
- "Ecosystem"
- "Synergy"

If you catch yourself using these, stop and rewrite in plain English.

---

## When In Doubt

Ask yourself: **"Would I say this out loud to someone?"**

If not, rewrite it.

The verbatim voice is how you'd explain your work to a smart colleague over coffee. Clear, direct, no BS.
